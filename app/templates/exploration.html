Exploration and Observations

Which months are road traffic accidents most likely to occur?

The heatmap shows how many accidents have occured for each day of the week in all calendar months. We can see that the months of September until December are characterized by a relatively high frequency of accident. These are the months of autumn in London, which are characterized by low temperature, fog and rain.

We also observe that, counterintuitively, fewer accidents take place in the chilly winter months of January and February. This might suggest that people are more reluctant to drive during this time.

What time of the day are road traffic accidents most likely to occur?

For each day of the week, this heatmap shows how many traffic accidents have occured by the hour. We observe that not surprisingly, most RTA happens during morning rush hour from 8am to 9am and evening rush hour from 3pm to 7pm (purple blocks). Friday is particularly worse compared to the other days of the week; this might be due to the fact that more people drive on Fridays.

How many vehicles are involved per accident?

The bar graph shows the distribution of accidents based on the number of vehicles involved. It shows that most accidents involve one or two vehicles. This intuitively makes sense; most accidents happen as a result of a car veering off the road and/or collision between two cars.

How many casualties are involved in the accidents?

The bar graph shows the distribution of accidents based on the number of casualties involved. A casualty can also be a minor injury to fatal. We see that most of the accidents involve only one casualty.

In which boroughs do RTAs happen most often?

The bar chart shows how many RTAs take place in different boroughs of London. We see that Westminster takes the number one spot and Kingston Upon Thames comes in last place with the least traffic accidents.

How does each borough rank in the number of road traffic accidents?

The map shows the same information as the bar chart above in a different way. It color codes the RTA probabilities of different boroughs, with accident-prone boroughs having darker colors. We can see that Westminster, with its intense purple color, taking the number one spot with the most traffic accidents. Kingston Upon Thames comes in last place with the least traffic accidents. The three most accident prones boroughs - Westminster, Barnet and Lambeth, are indicated with markers.
